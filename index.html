Types of Therapies in an acute hospital setting

  • Physical Therapy
  • Occupational Therapy
  • Speech and Language Pathology

These therapies are essential for patient recovery and rehabilitation. Each therapy focuses on different aspects of patient care to ensure a holistic approach. Collaboration among these therapies enhances patient outcomes. It's important that each patient understands the differences between these therapies to make informed decisions about their care.

For more information, read below.

Physical Therapy (PT)

Physical therapy in an acute care setting focuses on helping patients regain movement and manage pain after surgery, injury, or illness. Therapists work closely with patients to develop personalized treatment plans that promote recovery and improve quality of life.

Occupational Therapy (OT)

Occupational therapy helps patients develop or recover the skills needed for daily living and working. In an acute hospital setting, this may involve adapting tasks or environments to support patient independence and safety.

Speech and Language Pathology (SLP)

Speech and language therapy addresses communication and swallowing disorders. Therapists assess and treat patients to improve their ability to speak, understand language, and swallow safely.
